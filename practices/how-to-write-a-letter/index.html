The bottom line

Letter writing isn't a lost art — it's a neglected habit. The infrastructure is intact. What's faded is confidence. Write like you talk, start with where you are, ask questions you actually want answered, and include something physical. The recipient will remember it longer than any text.

Letter writing is not a lost art. It is a neglected one. The paper, the envelopes, the stamps, the postal service — all still exist, still work, still move things across distances for the price of a postage stamp. What has faded is the habit and the confidence.

Start With Where You Are

Literally. Where are you as you write this? What is the light like? What can you hear? This grounds the letter in a specific moment in time and immediately distinguishes it from an email. A letter that begins with the writer noticing something about their immediate surroundings feels alive in a way that a letter beginning with a status update does not.

"A letter that begins with the writer noticing something about where they are right now feels alive in a way that email never does."

Write Like You Talk (Slightly Better)

The reason people feel paralyzed by letter writing is that they try to write like a letter rather than like themselves. You don't need to be formal. You don't need to have Important Things to Say. Write the way you'd talk to this person if they were sitting across from you — with the small observations, the half-formed thoughts, the questions you genuinely want answered.

Ask Real Questions

The best letters generate correspondence. Ask questions you actually want answered, not questions you're asking to be polite. What are you reading right now? What did the winter feel like where you are? Is there something you've been thinking about that you haven't told anyone yet?

Include Something Physical

A pressed flower. A photo printed at a drugstore. A recipe cut from a magazine. A drawing your child made. Something physical tucked into the envelope transforms a letter into an artifact. The recipient keeps it. Years later, finding it in a box, they remember you.

The Tools Matter

You don't need expensive stationery. But writing on paper you like, with a pen you enjoy, makes the process more pleasurable and the result more considered. A fountain pen slows you down in exactly the right way.

Where to Find Pen Pals

IGGPPC (International Geek Girl Pen Pals Club), Slowly (a pen pal app), Reddit's r/penpals, and simply asking in your own community. The Writers Gone Wild community has letter writers in it — reach out if you want to connect with someone who will actually write back.
