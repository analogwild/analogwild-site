The bottom line

If you write in genuinely wet conditions: Rite in the Rain, no question. For everything else: Leuchtturm1917 A5 dotted for extended writing, Field Notes for pocket carry. The Baron Fig is for those who sketch alongside their writing.

There is a particular kind of notebook that belongs outside — one that can handle a light drizzle, survive a backpack pocket, and still be worth writing in at the end of a long day on the trail.

Rite in the Rain — Best for Serious Outdoor Use

Makes notebooks specifically for outdoor conditions. The paper sheds water rather than absorbing it — you can write in the rain, drop it in a puddle, and the ink holds. Available in field-book sizes that fit in a shirt pocket. Essential for anyone who takes notes in genuinely wet conditions.

Leuchtturm1917 A5 — Best All-Purpose Nature Journal

The standard recommendation for a reason. Dot grid is light enough to disappear at a distance while still guiding your writing and sketching. Numbered pages, index, pocket in the back. Not waterproof, but durable enough for careful outdoor use.

Shop: Leuchtturm1917 A5 on Amazon

Baron Fig Confidant — For the Sketch-Writers

If you combine writing and drawing, the Baron Fig has some of the best paper in the business for mixed media — handles pencil, fountain pen, and light watercolor without complaint. The lay-flat binding is excellent for sketching.

Field Notes — For Every Pocket

Small (3.5" x 5.5"), inexpensive, and designed to be carried everywhere. Having a small notebook in every jacket pocket changes how much you write down when you're outside. Field Notes also release limited seasonal editions with different paper types and covers.

Shop: Field Notes on Amazon

How to Start a Nature Journal

There are no rules. The most useful entries typically include: date, location, weather and light conditions, what you observed, a quick sketch, and anything that surprised you. A scratchy drawing of a bird you couldn't quite see is more valuable than a perfect photograph you took without really looking.
