The bottom line

The Leuchtturm1917 A5 dotted is our top pick for morning pages — numbered pages, a useful index, and paper that holds up to fountain pens. The Moleskine is a close second if you prefer ruled lines. Field Notes if you want something you can take anywhere without worrying about it.

Julia Cameron says the format doesn't matter. Write on printer paper if that's what you have. She's right, and also: it matters a little. The right notebook makes you want to open it. The wrong one makes morning pages feel like homework.

We've been doing morning pages — three longhand pages every morning, no agenda, no editing — for over a year across a rotating roster of notebooks. Here's the honest verdict.

Leuchtturm1917 — Our Top Pick

The Leuchtturm1917 A5 has everything morning pages needs: numbered pages (so you can actually use the index), a table of contents in the front, two ribbon bookmarks, and an expandable pocket in the back. The paper is 80gsm — light enough that the book doesn't feel like a brick, heavy enough to handle most pens without bleed-through.

The dot grid option is particularly good for morning pages. The dots give just enough structure to keep your lines straight without boxing you in. After filling seventeen of these, it's still the notebook we reach for first.

Moleskine Classic — The Reliable Workhorse

The Moleskine is the notebook everyone has heard of, and it earned that reputation. The paper is smooth, the binding is sturdy, and the elastic closure keeps everything tidy. Our one complaint: the paper is thinner than the Leuchtturm — 70gsm — which means fountain pens bleed through on the reverse side.

If you write with a ballpoint or pencil, this isn't an issue. If you've caught the fountain pen bug, go Leuchtturm.

Shop: Moleskine Classic on Amazon

Field Notes — For the Reluctant Morning Pager

Field Notes are small (3.5" x 5.5"), sturdy, and feel like nothing if you put them in a pocket. If you're new to morning pages and the idea of committing to three full pages feels daunting, start here. The smaller format means three pages goes faster, and there's something liberating about a notebook you don't feel precious about.

The downside: you'll go through them quickly, and the paper doesn't handle wet ink particularly well. But for getting the habit started, they're excellent.

Shop: Field Notes on Amazon

Rhodia — The Paper Obsessive's Choice

Rhodia's paper is exceptional — 90gsm, impossibly smooth, a genuine pleasure to write on. If you've developed opinions about paper (you'll know when this happens), Rhodia is where you end up. The dot grid pads are particularly satisfying.

The spiral binding on some versions can be annoying for left-handers. The hardcover Webnotebook avoids this issue and is the format we'd recommend.

Shop: Rhodia Webnotebook on Amazon

How to Choose

Use a fountain pen? Leuchtturm1917 or Rhodia. Both handle wet ink without bleed-through.

New to morning pages? Start with Field Notes — low commitment, easy to carry, goes fast.

Want the classic morning pages experience? Moleskine Large ruled. It's what most people picture when they imagine the practice.

Like to track and index? Leuchtturm1917 — the numbered pages and index make it uniquely useful over time.
