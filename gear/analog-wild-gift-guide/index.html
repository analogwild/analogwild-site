The bottom line

The best analog gifts are things the recipient would enjoy but wouldn't buy themselves — a fountain pen they've never tried, a beautiful notebook, a sun print kit, a board game they'd love. Include a handwritten note. The note will be remembered longer than the gift.

The best gifts for people who have everything tend to be things they wouldn't buy themselves — beautiful, a little indulgent, tactile, and analog. This guide is organized by recipient so you can find the right thing quickly.

For Writers ($10–$80)

Pilot Metropolitan Fountain Pen ($20) — The perfect introduction to fountain pens. Smooth, reliable, real metal construction.

Shop: Pilot Metropolitan on Amazon

Leuchtturm1917 Notebook ($22) — The notebook serious writers reach for. Numbered pages, index, quality paper.

Shop: Leuchtturm1917 on Amazon

Hobonichi Techo Planner ($25) — The small Japanese daily planner on extraordinary Tomoe River paper.

The Artist's Way by Julia Cameron ($16) — The book that has unlocked more writers than perhaps any other.

Shop: The Artist's Way on Amazon

For Outdoor People ($15–$150)

Field Notes 3-pack ($12) — Small, durable, fits in a back pocket. For the person who wants to write things down outside.

Shop: Field Notes on Amazon

Rite in the Rain Notebook ($10-15) — Waterproof paper for people who spend time in genuinely wet conditions.

Shop: Rite in the Rain on Amazon

Red headlamp for stargazing ($25-40) — Preserves night vision. A practical gift that enables one of the best free activities on earth.

Shop: Red Headlamp on Amazon

Disposable camera ($15) — For the outdoor person who documents everything on their phone.

Shop: Kodak Disposable Camera on Amazon

For Kids ($8–$40)

Sun print paper kit ($12-20) — The most magical craft material that exists. Works for ages 4+.

Shop: Sun Print Paper Kit on Amazon

Origami paper and beginner book ($10-15) — Hours of tactile engagement. The skill compounds.

Nature journal kit ($20-30) — A small notebook, colored pencils, and a guide to nature journaling.

For the Skeptic ($10–$50)

For the people in your life who are glued to their phones and respond best to a gift that doesn't lecture them about it.

A beautiful novel ($15-20) — Physical book. Something you actually read and loved. Include a handwritten note about why.

Codenames board game ($25) — Gets phones off the table. Funny, fast, works for almost any group.

Shop: Codenames on Amazon

Wingspan board game ($55) — Converts non-gamers reliably. Beautiful components, gentle gameplay.

Shop: Wingspan on Amazon

A Note on Gift-Giving

The most analog gift is your attention. A handwritten letter included with any of these will be remembered longer than the gift itself.
