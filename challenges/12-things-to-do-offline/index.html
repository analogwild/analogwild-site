12 Things to Do This Weekend With Your Phone in a Drawer

The bottom line

"Put your phone down" is only half the advice. The other half is: here's what to do instead. This list assumes you have a weekend, some basic supplies, and a willingness to remember what it feels like to be a person in the world.

The problem with "digital detox" advice is that it tells you what to stop without telling you what to start. Nature abhors a vacuum, and so do humans. Here are twelve specific things to fill the time — one for each waking hour of a good analog Saturday.

Morning

1. Morning pages. Three longhand pages before you look at anything. Julia Cameron's method. Twenty minutes. You will feel different after.

Shop: Leuchtturm1917 Notebook on Amazon

2. Make breakfast from a physical cookbook. Open to a random page. Make that. The constraint is the point.

3. Take a film walk. Load a roll of film (or grab a disposable camera) and walk your neighborhood like you're seeing it for the first time. You have 24 frames. Use them slowly.

Shop: Kodak Disposable Camera on Amazon

4. Write a letter. Pick one person. Write them a real letter — where you are, what you're noticing, a question you actually want answered. Put it in an envelope. Mail it.

"Put your phone down is only half the advice. Here's the other half."

Afternoon

5. Visit a library or independent bookstore. Wander. Buy one thing you wouldn't have found online.

6. Plant something. Even if it's just an herb in a pot on a windowsill. The act of putting something in soil and attending to it is quietly satisfying in a way that has no digital equivalent.

7. Make sun prints. Sun print paper, some leaves or flowers, sunlight, water. The whole thing takes 30 minutes and the results look like art.

Shop: Sun Print Paper Kit on Amazon

8. Read outside. Physical book. Somewhere with actual weather — wind, light, sounds. Read until you get cold or hungry or finish the chapter. Whichever comes first.

Evening

9. Play a board game. One that requires actual thinking or laughing. Codenames, Ticket to Ride, Wingspan. Invite someone over.

10. Cook something with your hands. Bread, pasta, anything that requires kneading or rolling or mixing. Not a recipe from your phone — from a book, or from memory, or made up.

11. Go somewhere dark and look up. If you're near anywhere with real darkness: go. Bring layers and something hot to drink. Stay long enough for your eyes to adjust.

12. Write down three things that happened. Not a gratitude list. Just three real things from the day. Evidence that it happened. Takes five minutes and becomes valuable in ways you won't expect.

The One Rule

The phone goes in a drawer Friday night. It comes out Sunday afternoon. Everything else follows from that.
