The bottom line

Absence of wifi is increasingly the luxury. Every place on this list offers genuine disconnection — no cell service, no televisions — plus accommodation that makes the experience worth the price. Budget picks start around $150/night; the aspirational ones go much higher.

Off-grid doesn't have to mean uncomfortable. Some of the best accommodation in the American West happens to have no cell service, no televisions, and no wifi — and they charge accordingly, because the absence of those things is increasingly what people are willing to pay for.

Oregon

Lake of the Woods Resort, Southern Oregon — Rustic cabins on a mountain lake near Crater Lake. No cell service, paddleboards, stars that will rearrange your priorities. From ~$150/night.

Suttle Lodge, Sisters OR — Lakeside cabins, no TVs by design, fireplace bar, mountains everywhere. Close enough to Bend to extend a weekend trip. From $200/night.

"The absence of wifi is increasingly the luxury. People are paying to not be reachable."

California

Mendocino Grove, Mendocino CA — Luxury canvas tents in a redwood grove above the coast. Heated beds, private decks, the sound of the Pacific. From $250-350/night.

AutoCamp Russian River, Guerneville CA — Airstream trailers in a redwood forest. Surprisingly private, Sonoma wine country nearby. From $200/night.

Utah

Under Canvas Zion, Springdale UT — Safari-style tents at the entrance to Zion. Stargazing, red rock sunrises. From $200/night.

Amangiri, Canyon Point UT — The splurge of all splurges ($2000+/night). Extraordinary desert architecture, canyon silence, a pool that reflects the sky. Once in a lifetime.

Montana

The Resort at Paws Up, Greenough MT — Luxury tents on 37,000 acres. Fly fishing, horseback riding, guided wilderness. From $500/night, all-inclusive.

Colorado

Dunton River Camp, Dolores CO — Remote canvas cabins along the Dolores River, accessible only by Jeep. Complete disconnection, exceptional food, the kind of quiet that resets something. From $400/night.

What to Pack

Good layers — nights are cold even in summer at elevation. A headlamp. A physical book. A journal. Your phone on airplane mode if you must bring it at all.
